Sri Lankan Star Tortoise

The Sri Lankan star tortoise is known for its
stunning shell pattern, which resembles
a star due to the radiating yellow lines on
each scute (shell segment).

  • Scientific Name: Geochelone elegans
  • Average Length: 25 to 30 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 50 to 80 years
  • Habitat: Dry, arid regions of Sri Lanka

The Sri Lankan star tortoise is a small to medium-sized tortoise native to the dry forests and scrublands of Sri Lanka. It is known for its distinctively patterned carapace (shell), which features radiating star-like patterns that vary in color from yellow to brown or black. They primarily feed on vegetation and require warm, dry habitats with access to sunlight for basking. Sri Lankan star tortoises are classified as vulnerable due to habitat loss and illegal pet trade, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival in the wild.
